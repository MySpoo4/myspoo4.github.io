2. System's Description & Core Features üíª

The web application is a professional job board designed to serve two primary actors: Recruiters (posting jobs) and Seekers (finding and applying to jobs). The focus of Sprint 1 was establishing the core user roles and fundamental search capabilities.

Job Seeking and Filtering

Homepage Search: Unregistered users and Seekers can search jobs by Title, Location, or Type directly on the homepage.

Advanced Filtering: The "Find Jobs" tab offers extra criteria: Experience Level and Salary Range.

Application Flow: Users can apply by filling out a form when clicking a job listing, and track their history in the top right.

Recruiter and Admin Management

Job Posting: Recruiters use the "Post Job" tab to enter all necessary details (title, company, description, salary, etc.).

Job Management: Recruiters can navigate to a specific page to edit or delete their posted jobs.

Admin Oversight: Administrators can view all job roles and manage both job posts and users via the dedicated Admin page.

Profile System

The Profile Page (top right access) displays critical professional info (role, skills, education, experience, links, bio). Users can edit their profile. Crucially, Recruiters can be contacted via email directly from their profile page.

3. Process Description & Design Rationale ‚öôÔ∏è

Our methodology was guided by Scrum, complemented by specific architectural decisions to ensure system integrity and efficiency.

Scrum Implementation (Sprint 1)

  • Backlog & Planning: We developed a product backlog of user stories and held an Initial Planning Meeting to select a critical chunk for Sprint 1 (authentication, core profiles, and basic job flow).
  • Communication: We maintained a continuous communication cycle by posting Scrum Standups to Microsoft Teams roughly every 1-2 days, allowing the Scrum Master (Warren) to quickly address any impediments.
  • Review: The sprint will conclude with a Sprint Review (Demo) with our client (TA) to gather feedback on the deployed features.

Design Decision: Technology Choice

We selected Django for the backend framework. This was a direct decision based on our assumption that the project required the same technology stack as a previous assignment, leveraging existing team familiarity to expedite Sprint 1 delivery.
